Tips to Save on Delivery Fees

💡 Use subscription plans. GrabFood and Foodpanda both offer monthly subscriptions (₱99–₱199/month) that waive delivery fees on eligible orders — great if you order frequently.
💡 Order during promos. Most apps offer free delivery vouchers during lunch hours (11am–1pm) or via their in-app rewards system.
💡 Meet minimum order thresholds. Some restaurants offer free delivery when you hit a minimum spend (usually ₱300–₱500). Check the restaurant page before adding to cart.
💡 Compare apps before ordering. The same restaurant may appear on both GrabFood and Foodpanda with different delivery fees depending on your distance from the branch.

Which Delivery App is Cheapest in the Philippines?

For food delivery, Foodpanda and LalaFood tend to offer the lowest base delivery fees, especially with promo codes. For package delivery, J&T Express and Ninja Van consistently offer the most affordable rates for nationwide shipping.

If you need same-day or on-demand courier service within Metro Manila, GrabExpress and Lalamove are the fastest options with competitive rates.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average food delivery fee in the Philippines?
The average delivery fee ranges from ₱29 to ₱75 depending on your distance from the restaurant and the platform you use.

Does GrabFood have free delivery in the Philippines?
Yes, GrabFood subscribers with GrabUnlimited or GrabFood Monthly Pass get free delivery on qualifying orders. Promo codes also offer free delivery regularly.

What is the cheapest courier in the Philippines?
J&T Express and Ninja Van are generally the most affordable couriers for e-commerce and personal parcel delivery nationwide.

How do I avoid high delivery fees?
Use subscription plans, apply promo codes, order above the free-delivery minimum, or choose a restaurant that's closer to your location.
